|{{Indent}}This is the ability to diagnose and fix ordinary mechanical problems. A successful skill roll will let you find or repair one problem

{{Indent}}You must pick a specialty from within one of these four categories:

{{Indent}}&lt;i&gt;Machine Type:&lt;/i&gt; Any one class of nonvehicular machine. Types include Micromachines (miniature machinery, invisible to the naked eye; TL9+), Nanomachines (molecular-scale machinery; TL10+), and Robotics (robots and automated factories; TL7+)

{{Indent}}&lt;i&gt;Motive System Type:&lt;/i&gt; Any one type

{{Indent}}of propulsion system, regardless of

{{Indent}}vehicle type. Types include Legged,

{{Indent}}Tracked, Wheeled, Rockets, and

{{Indent}}Reactionless Thrusters

{{Indent}}&lt;i&gt;Power Plant Type:&lt;/i&gt; Any one type

{{Indent}}of power plant, no matter what it

{{Indent}}powers. Types include Clockwork,

{{Indent}}Steam Engine, Gasoline Engine,

{{Indent}}Diesel Engine, Gas Turbine, Fuel Cell,

{{Indent}}Fission Reactor, Fusion Reactor, and

{{Indent}}Antimatter Reactor

{{Indent}}&lt;i&gt;Vehicle Type:&lt;/i&gt; The controls, hull,

{{Indent}}motive system, power plant, transmission,

{{Indent}}and even the paint job of one specific

{{Indent}}type of vehicle listed under a vehicle-

{{Indent}}operation skill such as Driving

{{Indent}}(p. 188), Piloting (p. 214), or

{{Indent}}Submarine (p. 223)

{{Indent}}Mechanic specialties default to one

{{Indent}}another at -4, although the GM may

{{Indent}}modify this for particularly close or distant

{{Indent}}specialties. The systems covered by

{{Indent}}each specialty vary by TL. For instance,

{{Indent}}Mechanic (Light Airplane) covers single-

{{Indent}}engine biplanes at early TL6, small

{{Indent}}private jets at TL7, and so forth

{{Indent}}Familiarity is very important here

{{Indent}}For instance, Mechanic7 (Light

{{Indent}}Airplane) covers both propeller-powered

{{Indent}}seaplanes and small private jets,

{{Indent}}but going from one to the other gives

{{Indent}}you -2 for an unfamiliar item (proppowered

{{Indent}}plane to jet) and -2 for an

{{Indent}}unfamiliar implementation (seaplane

{{Indent}}to regular plane), for a net -4 to skill

{{Indent}}until you familiarize yourself with all

{{Indent}}the differences
