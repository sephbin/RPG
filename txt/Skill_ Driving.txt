|{{Indent}}This is the ability to drive a specific type of ground vehicle

{{Indent}}Make an IQ-based Driving roll for basic map reading, to diagnose simple malfunctions, or to recall rules of the road

{{Indent}}You must specialize:

{{Indent}}&lt;i&gt;Automobile:&lt;/i&gt; Any vehicle with three or more wheels that weighs less than 5 tons and does not move on rails. &lt;i&gt;Defaults:&lt;/i&gt; Heavy Wheeled-2 or other Driving at -4

{{Indent}}&lt;i&gt;Construction Equipment:&lt;/i&gt; Any kind of bulldozer, crane, plow, etc. &lt;i&gt;Default:&lt;/i&gt; other Driving at -5

{{Indent}}&lt;i&gt;Halftrack:&lt;/i&gt; Any vehicle that moves on tracks and either wheels or skids. &lt;i&gt;Defaults:&lt;/i&gt; Tracked-2 or other Driving at -4

{{Indent}}&lt;i&gt;Heavy Wheeled:&lt;/i&gt; Any vehicle with three or more wheels that weighs 5 tons or more and does not move on rails. &lt;i&gt;Defaults:&lt;/i&gt; Automobile-2 or other Driving at -4

{{Indent}}&lt;i&gt;Hovercraft:&lt;/i&gt; Any kind of air-cushion vehicle. &lt;i&gt;Default:&lt;/i&gt; other Driving at -5

{{Indent}}&lt;i&gt;Locomotive:&lt;/i&gt; Any vehicle that moves on conventional or maglev rails. &lt;i&gt;Default:&lt;/i&gt; other Driving at -5

{{Indent}}&lt;i&gt;Mecha:&lt;/i&gt; Any kind of legged, bouncing, rolling, or slithering vehicle. &lt;i&gt;Defaults:&lt;/i&gt; Battlesuit-3 or other Driving at -5

{{Indent}}&lt;i&gt;Motorcycle:&lt;/i&gt; Any powered one- or two-wheeled vehicle, including those with sidecars. Large motorcycles often have a Minimum ST, just like a weapon (see p. 270). &lt;i&gt;Default:&lt;/i&gt; Bicycling-4

{{Indent}}&lt;i&gt;Tracked:&lt;/i&gt; Any vehicle that moves on tracks. &lt;i&gt;Defaults:&lt;/i&gt; Halftrack-2 or other Driving at -4

{{Indent}}Note that the ability to &quot;drive&quot; a team of animals is not Driving, but Teamster (p. 225)

{{Indent}}&lt;font color=&quot;#006AFF&quot; size=&quot;2&quot;&gt;&lt;i&gt;Modifiers: -2 or more for bad driving conditions; -2 or more for a vehicle in bad repair; -2 for an unfamiliar control system (e.g., an automatic when you are used to a manual); -4 or more for a vehicle of an unfamiliar type within your specialty (e.g., a race car when you are used to stock cars).&lt;/i&gt;&lt;/font&gt;
